Parts Washing

General Cleaning Information

  • Always wear protective gloves while handling cleaned parts
  • Check parts for burrs, scratches or any other damage
    • If a defect is found, notify Supervisor of problem

Methods Used For Cleaning Parts

Large Parts Washer

The large parts washer is used for cleaning large, heavy parts. This includes castings, billet cylinders, heads etc.

Opertation of Large Parts Washer
  • Individual Large Parts
  • Load part or parts into the washer
  • Set the parts on clean wood or plastic apparatus (free from imbedded chips) to ensure that machined surfaces will not be damaged.
  • Parts should be washed for approximately 1 - 5 minutes or long enough to remove metalworking fluids, grease, dirt, etc.
  • Rinse with water and dry with compressed air immediately
  • It is especially important to lightly and quickly rinse and then thoroughly dry cast iron parts then immediately saturate them with Stoddard solvent in order to prevent rust
  • For any part with a honed bore, it is very important to remove any grit or abrasive left from honing process by wetting a clean paper towel with Stoddard solvent and thoroughly wiping the bore until no trace of residue appears on towel
  • Place clean, dry parts on a cart lined with new cardboard and or new paper towels

Ultrasonic

Use for smaller and or delicate parts (valves, oil scrapers, etc.)

  • Fill ultrasonic cleaner with a solution of 10% to 20% Simple Green and 80% to 90% hot water
  • Turn ultrasonic on and load it with as many parts that will fit without damaging the surfaces. Cleaning time is approximately 5 minutes.
  • Rinse the parts in a clean bucket filled with hot water
  • Use compressed air to dry parts after cleaning process is complete
